## 1. Noncommercial makerspace self-host grant

A **noncommercial makerspace** — one run by a nonprofit, charity, community
group, club, school, or other organization that is **not operated for profit** —
may self-host, run, modify, and adapt OSMM to operate its own space **at no
charge**.

This grant does **not** cover a makerspace operated as a **for-profit business**.
A commercial / for-profit makerspace — including one that funds itself through
paid memberships, workshop fees, or similar revenue as a for-profit venture — is
**commercial use** and needs a commercial license from OSMM-HQ. See
[COMMERCIAL.md](COMMERCIAL.md).

## 2. Own operations only — providing OSMM to others is commercial

The grant above covers using OSMM for your **own** makerspace's internal
operations. Providing OSMM to other organizations **commercially** requires a
commercial license from OSMM-HQ. This includes, for example:

- selling, reselling, or redistributing OSMM as a paid product;
- hosting OSMM as a paid or commercial service (SaaS) that other organizations
  sign in to or run their spaces through;
- operating a shared or multi-tenant OSMM instance for other makerspaces as a
  paid or managed service;
- bundling OSMM into a commercial product or offering.

(Purely noncommercial sharing of copies — modified or not — remains governed by
the PolyForm license's own Distribution and "Changes and New Works" terms.)
